# PART 1 · Brand Deep Analysis

## 1.1 Category

**Conscious Embodiment for Accomplished Souls.**

A category we are creating. It sits between premium wellness (Goop, Heckfield) and authentic spiritual lineage (Kaula tantra). Most creators inhabit one or the other. Authentic Tantra sits in the intersection.

What makes this category sellable as a media brand:
- **Visual restraint** + **spiritual depth** = rare combination
- **Founder credibility** (12 years executive business, 7 years daily 5am practice, retreats in Peru/Tibet/dark) — almost no one in Russian-speaking premium-feminine wellness has this spine
- **Russian + international flexibility** — Phangan-based but Moscow-rooted, bilingual presence

## 1.2 Brand archetype mix (locked)

- **60% Sage** — wisdom, lineage, restraint, the teacher who refuses to be a guru
- **40% Lover** — embodied, sensory, intimate, soft warmth

**What this means visually:**
- Sage shows up in: lineage references, quiet typography, philosophical content, single-object compositions
- Lover shows up in: skin, warmth, soft fabric, slow movement, candle light, two-cups-on-windowsill

**What it is NOT:**
- Magician (esoteric/mystical) — no chakras, no kundalini, no sacred geometry
- Outlaw (provocative) — no shock-value, no "open your wild feminine"
- Hero (achievement) — no "transform in 30 days," no "become your highest self"

## 1.3 Who pays

| Avatar | Visual triggers | What stops scroll |
|---|---|---|
| **А1** Анна (women, 32-44, accomplished) | recognition, soft permission, intimate texture | "she's seeing me without exposing me" |
| **А2** Couples (35-50, post-honeymoon) | maturity codes, two-of-something, no faces | "we're in this together, it's allowed to be quiet" |
| **А3** Алексей (skeptics, 30-48, tech/business) | precision, science references, business spine | "she's not selling me a fantasy" |

## 1.4 The unfair advantage (do not dilute)

1. **Real Kaula lineage** — most "tantra teachers" learned at weekend workshops
2. **12 years business spine** — credibility bridge for А3 + Forbes-aspirational А1
3. **Lives the practice daily** (5am, 7 years) — not posed
4. **Already-built infrastructure** — website, content system, channels, audience

## 2.2 Typography (locked)

- **Display + body italic:** Cormorant Garamond (Light 300, Light Italic 300)
- **UI + small caps:** Inter (Light 300, Regular 400, Medium 500)

## 2.3 Image library tonal rules

Every image we make or shoot must pass these three:
1. **One light source** (window, candle, sun) — never flat or multi-light
2. **One subject anchor** (face, hand, cup, doorway) — never crowded
3. **Cream / warm earth tone** wash overall — never cool blues or greens dominating

# PART 3 · Visual Creative Direction (the full sensory brief)

## 3.1 Mood

**Three words:** *quiet · ripening · returning*

Not "ethereal." Not "magical." Not "transformative."

Quiet = the absence of performance. The video doesn't try to win you.
Ripening = something developing slowly inside the frame (a face breathing, a candle burning down, fabric moving).
Returning = the recurring motif of going back — to body, to breath, to a moment before. Visually: pulling-in shots, hand returning to heart, returning home.

## 3.2 Lighting

**Default:** single warm source from one direction. Never flat.

- **Morning light** (5-9am) — long horizontal warm rays through a window, deep shadows
- **Candle light** (any time) — small intimate pool of warm light, deep surrounding darkness
- **Dawn ambient** (just-before-sunrise) — cool blue washed with first warm — most magical 20 minutes/day
- **Late afternoon** (4-6pm) — golden hour, raking horizontal sun
- **Avoid:** midday sun (too harsh), overhead office light (clinical), neon/screen light (cheap), HDR filters

**Color temperature:** 2700-3500K warm. Never above 4500K (cool). Never blue-tinted.

## 3.3 Camera style

**Reference:** Sofia Coppola × Andrea Arnold × Bill Henson.

- **Stillness over movement** — locked-off camera, single takes, no quick cuts
- **Observational distance** — like watching from across the room, not interviewing
- **Closer than expected** — when we do close-up, very close (eyelashes, breath, fabric texture)
- **Lower angles** — slightly below eye line, never overhead drone-style
- **One unbroken second per 4 seconds of cut** — if cut, the cut breathes
- **35mm or 50mm equivalent** — never fisheye/wide, never compressed telephoto
- **Slow-motion only when justified** — falling fabric, slow breath visible, hand reaching — not for sentiment

## 3.4 Colors (translated for photo/video grade)

- Highlights: cream / pearl / champagne
- Midtones: warm earth, taupe, soft pink-grey, sand
- Shadows: charcoal, deep brown, never pure black
- **Saturation:** desaturated 15-25% from camera default — closer to film than digital
- **Contrast:** moderate, never crushed shadows or blown highlights

## 3.5 Textures (visible, please)

The image should feel touchable. Active textures:
- Linen (rumpled, lived-in, not pressed)
- Raw silk (champagne, sage, cream — never bright color)
- Bare wood (oak, walnut — visible grain)
- Plaster walls (irregular, organic)
- Wax (melting candle, rivulets)
- Smoke / steam (slow rising — implies time)
- Paper (book pages, handwritten notes)
- Skin (without makeup overlay — visible texture)

## 3.6 Atmosphere

**Air should feel still but alive.** Like the air in a quiet room with one window open.

- Dust particles visible in light shaft = yes (only if natural)
- Steam from tea = yes
- Hair moving in slight breeze = yes
- Background completely sharp = no — slight bokeh, always
- Multiple things competing for attention = no

## 3.7 Composition

Three rules:
1. **One subject, lots of breath around it** (negative space ≥ 60% of frame)
2. **Asymmetric, never centered** (rule of thirds, but pulled further to edge)
3. **Eye line natural, never camera-forward** (when face is in frame, eyes look down or to the side 90% of the time; direct camera only at key narrative moments)

## 3.8 Emotions to evoke (per viewer state)

| Pillar | Target emotion | Anti-emotion |
|---|---|---|
| Lineage / Demystification | sober certainty, "she knows" | mysticism, "magic" |
| Embodied Practice | softening, exhale | obligation, effort |

| Relationship | warmth, hope, tender re-meeting | conflict, prescription |
| Science Bridge | intellectual relief, "permission" | overwhelm, jargon |

## 3.11 Environment

**Default environments:**

- **Phangan (her home):** bamboo + concrete, large windows, ocean adjacent, soft white linen, single chair, low platform bed
- **Moscow (when there):** plaster walls, parquet wood floor, single window with morning light, books visible
- **Retreat settings:** Peru (wood + plant medicine altar), Bali (open-air pavilion), other Asia
- **Universal "practice room":** bare wood floor, one chair, one candle, one window, one book, one cup

**Avoid:** crowded urban scenes, busy cafes, gyms, modern minimalist sterile (Apple Store aesthetic), corporate offices.

## 3.12 Facial expressions

This is the most important visual law for Victoria.

**Direct camera:** rare, used 10% of frames. When used, **no smile**. Slight breath. Eyes neutral or warm.

**Off-camera (looking down, to side, eyes closed):** 90% of frames. This is the brand's signature — she's not performing.

**Never:** big smile, posed laughter, performative joy, theatrical sadness, "spiritual eyes-rolled-back," eyes closed in pretend bliss.

**Often:** half-smile (gentle, private), eyes down, eyes closed naturally (during practice), face partially turned away.

## 3.13 Movement

**Slow.** All movement, slow.

- Walking: 60% normal speed
- Pouring tea: lingering, full action
- Hand to chest: deliberate, no rush
- Sitting down: feel the chair receive
- Looking up: half a breath of pause first

In Reels: editorial slow-motion (60fps capture, played at 30fps) for movement clips. Native speed for talking-head.

# PART 4 · AI Image Prompt System

## 4.1 Universal prompt grammar (apply to all)

Every AI prompt must include these 7 elements in this order:

1. **Subject + action** (what is happening)
2. **Composition** (close-up / medium / wide, angle)
3. **Lighting** (single source, time of day, warmth)
4. **Environment** (room/outdoor/setting)
5. **Emotional quality** (one descriptor)
6. **Style references** (A24 + 1-2 photographers)
7. **Technical parameters** (aspect ratio, model version)

**Universal exclusions** (add to every prompt's negative): `no text, no logos, no chakras, no mandalas, no lotus, no Sanskrit, no crystals, no neon, no overhead lighting, no HDR, no oversaturation, no smiling, no posed expressions`

## 4.2 Aspect ratios by surface

| Surface | Aspect | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Instagram Feed photo | 4:5 | Vertical, takes most screen |
| Instagram Reel/Story | 9:16 | Vertical full |
| Carousel slide | 1:1 (1080×1080) | Square |
| Website hero | 16:9 | Landscape |
| YouTube thumbnail | 16:9 | Landscape |
| Telegram pinned image | 1:1 or 4:5 | Either works |

## 4.3 Midjourney prompt template (v7)

```
[subject + action], [composition + angle], [lighting + time of day], [environment with 2-3 texture descriptors], [emotional quality], shot on Mamiya 7 medium format with Kodak Portra 400 film, A24 cinematography style, editorial luxury wellness, soft natural shadows, Sofia Coppola lighting, slow living mood, warm cream and taupe palette, charcoal shadows, photographed by Sarah Moon and Bill Henson, intimate observational angle, no text, no logos, no symbols --ar 4:5 --style raw --v 7
```

## 4.4 Flux prompt template


# PART 5 · Carousel Design System

## 5.1 Two carousel modes

**Mode A — Poetry Carousel** (5-7 slides, sparse text, brand cream + Cormorant italic)
Use when: poetic content, slow emotional arc, save-bait single phrases.

**Mode B — Document Carousel** (10-15 slides, dense text, brand cream + Cormorant regular)
Use when: educational/argument content, save-bait reference, longer share-worthy.

## 5.2 Universal slide template

Every slide has:
- **Anchor:** thematic illustration (line-art, charcoal lines + brass accent) in top portion — 280×280 zone, 130px from top
- **Text:** centered Cormorant Garamond serif, brand-aligned size
- **Optional:** brass thin line as section divider (slide 6 of Poetry carousels)
- **Always:** subtle paper grain, eyebrow-margin breathing room

## 5.3 Per-slide pacing logic

**Poetry carousel of 7 slides:**

| Slide | Purpose | Emotional pace | Type of text |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Hook | open, intrigue | 4-8 words italic |
| 2 | Context | place reader | sentence in regular |
| 3 | Tension | name the friction | one short paragraph |
| 4 | Pivot | shift the frame | italic short |
| 5 | Recognition | confirm the truth | regular medium |
| 6 | Soft landing | offer the next | italic short |
| 7 | Closing breath | symbol or single word | one-word italic + brass line |

**Document carousel of 12 slides:**

| Slide | Purpose |
|---|---|
| 1 | Title with subtitle |
| 2 | Why this matters in one sentence |
| 3-10 | Numbered points (one per slide) |
| 11 | Synthesis sentence |
| 12 | Soft invitation (not "buy" — "if this resonates, ...") |

## 5.4 Typography rules per slide

- **Hook slide (1):** Italic, large (~96-102px on 1080×1080)
- **Body slides:** Regular, medium (~50-56px)
- **Pivot/poetic slides:** Italic, medium (~72-92px)
- **Final slide:** Italic, large (~96px), with brass line beneath

## 5.5 Transition logic (how slides flow)

- Cream → cream (no jarring color shifts between slides)
- Illustration motifs **rhyme** across slides (cup on slide 1, empty bowl on slide 3 — same family)
- Final slide always has slight pause-anchor (the brass line = visual full stop)

# PART 6 · Reels Direction System

## 6.1 Three Reel formats (locked)

| Format | Length | Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Teaching Reel** | 30-60s | Story, demystification, recognition, practice |
| **Lifestyle Reel** | 20-35s | Pillar 6 (Living Practice) — vignettes |
| **Confessional Reel** | 30-45s | Founder personal reveals |

## 6.2 Universal Reel shot list (Teaching)

| Sec | Shot | Voice | B-roll possibility |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00-0:02 | Wide cinematic establishing shot (no face) — window, candle, cup | None (silence) | Window, candle close-up |
| 0:02-0:08 | Cut to Victoria mid-shot, three-quarter angle, eyes down | Hook line | Hand reaching to cup |
| 0:08-0:25 | Lock on Victoria, close enough to see breath | Teaching | Inserts of hand, fabric, candle flame |
| 0:25-0:40 | Pull back, slower pacing | The pivot | Slow movement |
| 0:40-0:50 | Cut to environmental shot — doorway, sea, window | Closing line (voiceover possible) | Sun setting, light moving |
| 0:50-end | Final eye contact, half-second smile | Last word | None |

## 6.3 Camera angles per Reel type

- **Teaching:** 3/4 angle, slight low (looking slightly up), medium close
- **Lifestyle:** object-led wides + close-ups, never full face
- **Confessional:** straight-on, very close, often partial face

## 6.4 Transitions (the cinematic palette)

| Transition | When |
|---|---|
| Hard cut on action | Practice Reels — hand moves, cut |
| Cross-dissolve (~12 frames) | Memory / story Reels — past to present |
| Match cut (object → object) | Lifestyle Reels — cup → flower → window |
| Audio-led cut (cut on a breath) | All Reels — the cut breathes |

**Never:** swipe transitions, zoom transitions, flash cuts, beat-drop cuts, glitch transitions, popular TikTok templates.

## 6.5 B-roll motif library (build over time)

Shoot once, use across many Reels. Categories:

- **Hand motifs:** hand on chest, hand on cup, hand brushing grass, hand on book, two hands cupping (saver)
- **Object motifs:** candle lighting moment, tea pouring, book closing, door opening
- **Environment motifs:** window with morning light, doorway with backlight, walking shot from behind, slow camera move along a wall
- **Body motifs:** breath visible in chest, collarbone in morning light, foot on wood floor, hair moving in breeze, eyes closing

## 6.6 Music vibe

**Default:** silence + ambient (wind, distant birds, single low piano note, breath).

**When music is appropriate:**
- Solo piano (Nils Frahm, Olafur Arnalds, Max Richter low compositions)
- Single cello note (Hildur Guðnadóttir style)
- Ambient field recording (rain, ocean, wind)
- Tibetan bowl single tone at start or end (used once per month, ritual signal)

**Volume:** -25dB to -30dB. Music should never compete with voice.

**Never:** trending TikTok audio, lo-fi beats, motivational soundtracks, generic "spa music," "om" chanting, drum-circle audio.

## 6.7 Pacing per Reel type

- **Teaching:** medium — 1 cut per 4-6 seconds, with 1-2 longer holds
- **Lifestyle:** slow — 1 cut per 5-7 seconds, almost no movement within shot
- **Confessional:** very slow — sometimes 1 cut for the entire 30s, breath as the only motion

## 6.8 Emotional climax structure

Every Reel has one climax moment — the line or shot that earns the save/share.

| Position | Climax type |
|---|---|
| 0:08-0:12 (hook delivery) | "Wait, what?" intellectual hook |
| 0:25-0:35 (mid-Reel) | Most emotional sentence — pause before |
| 0:45-0:55 (closing) | The line that gets screenshot |

Plan it. Mark it. Edit around protecting it.

# PART 8 · Instagram Stories System

## 8.1 The daily 4-Stories arc

Every day, 4 stories at intervals:

| Time МСК | Slot | Purpose | Engagement device |

# PART 10 · Sales Psychology (how each element converts)

The brand sells by **lowering psychological cost of moving forward**, never by raising urgency.

## 10.1 The eight quiet conversion mechanisms

1. **Recognition before invitation.** Always name the state first ("Вы не устали — вы не в теле"), THEN invite. Never invite cold.

2. **Anti-guru positioning compounds.** Every "I'm not a teacher, I'm a fellow traveler" line reduces resistance for the А3 skeptic. Repeat across surfaces.

3. **Body proof of teaching.** Pillar 6 (Living Practice) content does sales work — viewers see Victoria living what she teaches, lowering scepticism without arguing.

4. **The slow ask.** First mention of 1:1 / Circle should be 4-6 weeks into someone's exposure. By then they've felt 12+ touches of value.

5. **Free door always open.** LM-CTA in every Reel caption = constant low-friction option. Even non-buyers stay in funnel.

6. **Premium positioning by restraint.** Aesop/Heckfield aesthetic itself signals "this costs more." Visual luxury → price acceptance.

7. **Founder availability calibrated.** "I read every application personally" + "2-3 days response time" — these phrases create premium AND scarcity AND warmth without using "limited spots" trick.

8. **Story drives 1:1 conversion. Practice drives Circle conversion. Demystification drives LM.** Each pillar has its primary conversion path. Don't confuse them.

## 10.2 Desire architecture (no manipulation)

Stack these in order of emotional pull, never violating brand:

1. **Belonging** — "you're not alone in this"
2. **Permission** — "you may stop trying"
3. **Recognition** — "I see what you've hidden"
4. **Intimacy** — "we are in this small circle together"
5. **Status** (subtle) — "the women in this Circle are the ones already done with surface work"
6. **Transformation** — "you will return to a body you forgot"

Only #6 is the "outcome." 1-5 are emotional access.

## 10.3 Exclusivity without arrogance

| What works | What breaks brand |
|---|---|
| "8 spots" (real number, intimate) | "Limited time" (creates urgency) |
| "Application-based" | "VIP" / "exclusive access" |
| "I read each one personally" | "Only for serious" |
| "Cohort starts when it's ready" | "Doors close Monday" |
| Premium price as filter | Discount as filter |

## 10.4 Emotional safety as conversion strategy

Phrases that create safety AND drive conversion:

- "Вы не одна."
- "Если внутри звучит «не сейчас» — это тоже ответ. Я остаюсь рядом."
- "Не нужно быть готовой."
- "Я не «диагностирую», не «исправляю». Я рядом, пока вы возвращаетесь."
- "Доступ к этой работе — не от усилия. От согласия."

These read as care AND remove psychological friction.

## 10.5 Premium positioning rules

Premium = scarcity + restraint + earned-status + sensory excellence.

- Use **specific numbers** not "many" (8 spots, not "small group")
- Use **specific places** not "around the world" (Phangan · Moscow · Peru)
- Use **specific years** not "long time" (12 years business, 7 years daily practice)
- Use **specific names** when crediting (Bessel van der Kolk, Stephen Porges)
- Show **single objects** in luxury context (one chair, not Pinterest mood-board)
- **Quiet typography over decorative** every time

# PART 13 · Document closing — the one thing

If everything in this document is forgotten except one principle, this:

**Restraint is the moat.**

Every premium spiritual creator in 2026 will be running A24-style aesthetics within 18 months. The thing that survives the imitation is **what we DON'T do**.

We don't smile on cue. We don't add mandalas. We don't promise transformation. We don't use exclamations. We don't show every chakra diagram our competitor uses. We don't translate "tantra" as "sexual technique."

The brand's competitive advantage isn't visual sophistication (everyone will have that). It's **the discipline to hold the line when everyone else is adding more**.

When in doubt — remove one thing.
