# Premium Experience Principles

Luxury in this brand is not thread-count. It is reverence made tangible.

## Sensory Signature
Every retreat has a consistent sensory signature the guest will remember:
- **Scent** — palo santo at arrival, cacao at circle, sandalwood in practice room
- **Sound** — handpan, singing bowl, long silences; never top-40
- **Light** — candles for evening, natural only for day, no overhead fluorescence
- **Touch** — handmade ceramics, linen, wood, stone; no plastic anywhere

## Premium Touchpoints
### Before arrival
- Handwritten welcome letter mailed to home address
- Curated playlist link for the travel day
- A single, considered ritual item sent (a small stone, a handmade candle)

### Arrival
- Victoria greets every guest personally at the door
- Room prepared with name card, a welcome note, a glass of rosewater
- No check-in desk. It is a home.

### During
- Slow service — meals are ceremonies, not events
- Daily "still hour" — no schedule, no practice, just held space
- Journal, pen, and body oil placed in each room at the start

### Before departure
- Integration conversation — 20 min 1:1 with Victoria or lead facilitator
- Gift: a photograph of the group, printed not digital

### After return
- Handwritten postcard from Victoria within 14 days of departure
- One-on-one integration call at day 30

## Price-to-Experience Ratio Rules
- If a guest pays €5,000, the experience must feel like €10,000 and cost like €2,000 to produce.
- Waste money on beauty. Save money on complexity.

## What guests never experience
- Cheesy "Welcome!" signs
- Branded merchandise (no hoodies, no tote bags, no water bottles with logos)
- Upsells during the retreat
- Social-media capture requests from Victoria's team
- "Can we film your testimonial?" moments

# Service Team Principles
- Every staff member is a practitioner of the work.
- Silent, precise, warm. No forced friendliness.
- Staff blesses the room before guests arrive and after they leave.
- Every room is cleaned in silence.
