# The Retreat Arc

Every retreat — 3-day intensive or 7-day immersion — follows the same five-phase
architecture. It is the container that holds everything.

## Phase 1 — Arrival (Прибытие) · 1 evening
**Purpose:** Shed the outer life.
**Design:**
- Silent welcome (no icebreakers).
- Warm meal prepared with intention.
- Opening circle at dusk — one sentence per participant: "What did you leave behind to come here?"
- Victoria tells Acts 1–5 of the founder story — the night's only teaching.
- Early sleep.

## Phase 2 — Descent (Спуск) · Days 2–3
**Purpose:** Move below the everyday self.
**Design:**
- Morning: silence until 10am. Body practice. Tea.
- Afternoon: teaching + deep practice.
- Evening: somatic work, breathwork, or darkness.
- **What we avoid:** group discussion loops, testimonials, anything intellectual.

## Phase 3 — Core Work (Глубина) · Days 4–5 (of a 7-day)
**Purpose:** The heart of the retreat — where remembering happens.
**Design:**
- Longer, more intimate practices.
- Possible ritual element (fire ceremony, offering).
- 1:1 time with Victoria built in.
- Space for tears, silence, unstructured being.

## Phase 4 — Integration (Интеграция) · Day 6
**Purpose:** Begin the return — with new substance.
**Design:**
- Morning: longer integration practice.
- Afternoon: writing hour (guided).
- Evening: sharing circle (bounded, facilitated).
- Meal together as community.

## Phase 5 — Closing (Закрытие) · Day 7
**Purpose:** Carry it home.
**Design:**
- Final practice at dawn.
- Closing circle: "What do you carry back?"
- Blessing ceremony.
- Practical: integration plan sheet, follow-up dates, sister/brother pairings.

# Non-Negotiables
- Maximum 25 participants per retreat. Ever.
- At least one 1:1 touch between Victoria and each participant.
- No photography on retreat except by designated photographer in specific windows.
- Phones collected on arrival evening, returned on departure morning (optional opt-out).
- Dietary care: plant-based, local, simple, prepared with love.

# Location Principles
- Natural setting — forest, sea, mountain. Never city.
- Privacy — no shared facilities with other groups.
- Beauty — physical space must carry reverence.
- Silence possible — the land must allow it.
